    Crisis management in adolescents with autism (To be confirmed)
    Prof. Mely Knott y Psic. Javier Sotomayor

    Summary: One of the strategies with the most successful results, used in the management of problematic situations (crisis) in the population that has received the diagnosis of autism, bases its principles on the integrated experience. This intervention can be defined by the implementation of four key strategies: 1) the search for the integration of a peaceful and positive experience for the adolescent; 2) revaluation of body language; 3) the meaning and clarity of the word; and 4) how the behavior of others will always be affected by ours.

    Self-harm in patients with Autism Spectrum Disorder
    LP Israel David Castro Ramos

    Summary: The talk will try to explain the behavioral problems that often the way of communication for those whose linguistic abilities are severely limited. Showing serious behavioral problems may be the only way to influence others: Self-harm can be motivated by obtaining concrete things, or by avoiding or escaping aversive situations or by getting the attention of people around. Some models that could explain in general terms self-injurious behaviors and which of this model could undergo the symptoms of Autism Spectrum Disorder will be explained.
